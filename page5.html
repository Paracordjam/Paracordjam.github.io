The history of the paracord braclet

Shortly before the Second World War, a polyamide called Nylon was invented by Wallace Carothers of DuPont. His invention made it possible to carry out a new kind of large-scale mobile airborne warfare. Parachutes have previously been made of silk, something very inadequate because of its natural manner, the humble silkworm. This synthetic silk has transformed a war by allowing parachutes and the accompanying product, parachute cord, to be produced massively.

The parachutist, who was driven behind enemy lines, needed multifunctional, functional, reliable and lightweight tools as far as possible. With their parachute cord, the soldiers found various and imaginative applications. Once at the ground, the parachute was frequently cut by parachutes and used for future purposes. They had been trained to use parachute string or parachute "paracord" in both survival and improvised repair situations.

Following World War II, the US military adopted paracord as a multi-use tool in a variety of applications for use throughout the Army. Today, it is still part of the military standard and is used to secure equipment for backpacks and trucks, for shelter construction and other improvisational solutions for many practical applications.

It is perhaps surprising that paracords still make an important role in the every-day life of man, as people have always used different types of cords for survival — constructing, measuring, fishing, moving (sailing, cars), etc.

How many uses you can receive from this extremely strong and durable cord is exceptional. Only claim NASA is a bit special about what you choose to put out in space where every g of weight is taken into account for its importance and usefulness and is re-considered. NASA believed in paracord value and made it an accessory for the missions of its astronauts. It was actually used for the improvided repair of the Hubble space telescope during the space shuttle mission. In order to make Naimakka Paracord bracelets the very same type and quality of paracord used in this space reparation.
